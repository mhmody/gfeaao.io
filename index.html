Manifesto for the Equal Expertise, Harmony Society - Selection of Board

  • Equal Rights and Inclusivity: We firmly believe in providing equal rights and opportunities to all members of our society, regardless of their background, profession, or social status. Every individual's voice and concerns will be heard and respected.

  • Adherence to Legal Obligations:We will uphold and strictly follow all legal rights and obligations of every member as per their respective legal deeds. Our committment to legality ensures a fair and just environment within the society.

  • Diverse Expertise: Our society comprises professionals from various fields, including Doctor, Scientist, Civil Engineer, Assistant Professor, Electrical Engineer, Mechanical Engineer, Chartered Accountant, Police Officer, Lawyer, and Social Workers. This diversity allows us to address the diverse needs of our community effectively.

  • Problem Solving through Expertise: We recognize that the society might face various challenges and issues. As board members, we will leverage our collective expertise to find innovative and practical solutions for any problem that arises.

  • Preserving Harmony: Harmony within the society is paramount to us. We will foster an environment where members can coexist peacefully, fostering a strong sense of community and mutual respect.

  • Transparency in Builder Dealings: With our expertise on the board, we will ensure that builders cannot take advantage of society members during the handover or takeover processes. Transparent dealings will be a priority.

  • Resisting Unfair Treatment: Recent events have shown that the dissolved Ad-hoc committee members have displayed a flagrant disrespect for their fellow members, using derogatory language that demeans and undermines the hard work and dedication of others. The term "BOSTIBASHI" that they used is not only offensive but reveals a disturbing attitude towards fellow members, suggesting they view themselves as superior and others as mere servants or subordinates. By allowing those who have shown such disregard for their peers to become board members, we risk compromising the very essence of our community.

  • Reduced Maintenance Charges: we will strive to reduce maintenance charges while ensuring the society's essential amenities and services are well-maintained.

  • Support for Non-Resident Members: We will take special care of non-resident members who wish to rent out their flats, ensuring a smooth and peaceful rental process.
